## 🎯 Objective

To understand the role of a Security Operations Center (SOC) analyst and how defenders monitor, detect, and respond to cyber threats.

## 🛠️ Tools Used

- SIEM dashboard (Security Information and Event Management)
- Security logs (firewall, endpoint, etc.)
- EDR (Endpoint Detection and 
  jResponse) – conceptual
- Incident response playbooks

## 🔍 What a SOC Analyst Does

They watch security alerts, investigate suspicious activity, decide which alerts are real threats, and escalate or respond according to company procedures.

## 🧠 Key Learning

I learned the difference between offensive (attack) and defensive (protect) security. A SOC analyst is a defender – the first line of defence against cyber attacks.

## 🌍 Why This Matters

Without defenders, an attacker could stay inside a company's network for months,staying unnoticed and stealing data or planting ransomware. SOC analysts help stop attacks early.

## Why is this important for a real company?  
Because attackers could be inside a network for weeks without anyone noticing. Defenders are the first line of detection.
 Without defensive security, a company is blind to attacks. Hackers could steal customer data, transfer money, or lock systems with ransomware – and no one would notice until it's too late. Defensive security (SOC analysts, SIEM monitoring, incident response) gives a company early warning, fast reaction, and protection against financial loss, legal fines, and damaged reputation. Every bank, hospital, and tech company needs defenders to stay safe.
